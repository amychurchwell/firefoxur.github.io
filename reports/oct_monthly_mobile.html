October Monthly Mobile Research

Date: October 2015

Products: Firefox Android & Firefox iOS

Location: United States

Researcher: Gemma Petrie


2015 marks the first year that the Firefox Mobile Team has had a dedicated user researcher. In October, we began a series of research projects currently referred to as "Monthly Mobile" (snappier title suggestions are welcome :). This is the first research project in this series.

Research Goals
  • Provide a regular opportunity for user feedback on Firefox for Android and Firefox for iOS.
  • Expose members of the Firefox Mobile team to real users on a regular basis.
  • Provide an overview of the Firefox mobile experience from the perspective of new Firefox for Android and Firefox for iOS users.
  • Providing direction for future Monthly Mobile research topics.
  • Establish a working set of documents, partners, and processes that will allow the Monthly Mobile research project to be regularly repeatable.
Methods
Diary Study:

Participants were asked to jot down a diary entry every time they used the browser on their device. Additionally, participants were asked to pay special attention to specific themes for each day:

  • Day 1: Welcome Screens, Tabs, Search
  • Day 2: Home Panels, Reading List, Settings, Sync
  • Day 3: Add-ons, Privacy, Help
  • USE Survey:  Participants also took part in a vetted usability questionnaire: USE. The USE survey contains items phrased as statements about Firefox and each response is measured on a 5-point agree-disagree scale. Therefore, a selection of 3 is the midpoint — meaning that below 3 is disagreement and above 3 is agreement
  • Interviews:  Following the diary study, 3 Firefox for Android and 2 Firefox for iOS participants were invited to take part in a one-hour video interview with Gemma and members of the Mobile team. Thank you Barbara, Chenxia, Darrin, Karen, Margaret, Maria & Robin!
Primary Findings

Welcome Screens:

  • Many participants are still not sure if they were offered any on boarding, yet most expect to see it.
  • Participants requested that we include (1) How to change the settings and (2) How to find/use basic features.
  • Other recommended additions based on this research: Home panels, quick search, reading list.

Search:

  • For some users, Yahoo as default is a barrier. Is it possible to allow people to pick their provider during on boarding?
  • During our interviews, only one participant even noticed the quick search options. How can we highlight these better?

    • Settings:

    • Consider adding a reset button for less savvy users that are worried about changing the wrong thing.
